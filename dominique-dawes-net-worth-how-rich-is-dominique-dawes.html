Who Is Dominique Dawes?

Dominique Dawes is a retired American artistic gymnast who competed as part of the “Magnificent Seven,” the U.S. women’s gymnastics team during the 1996 Atlanta Olympics.

Dawes was born on November 20, 1976, in Maryland, United States, and began her gymnastics career at age 10.

She represented the United States from 1989 to 2000, and emerged the U.S. all-around senior National Champion in 1994.

Dawes was known for her exceptional skills on the uneven bars and floor exercise.

She won several medals at the World Championship, and became a three-time Olympian, making her one of only three female American gymnasts to compete in three Olympics.

She is also the first African American woman to win an individual Olympic gymnastics medal, earning a bronze in the floor exercise at the 1996 Games.

Dawes retired from competitive gymnastics in 2000. Upon retirement, she remained active in the gymnastics community as a coach, commentator, and advocate.
