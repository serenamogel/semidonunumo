Discovering Solna: 7 Exciting Activities for USA Travelers

Situated somewhat north of Stockholm City Center, Solna Municipality is a municipality and principal settlement in Stockholm, Sweden.

The town of Solna, which is a part of the Stockholm metropolitan region, serves as its seat. One of Sweden’s wealthiest municipalities is Solna.

Cohesive and energetic, Solna evolves and thrives in a way that benefits all of its residents. The city is a free, open, and safe place that respects the potential of its citizens. For families living abroad, Solna is a great option.

Situated about one hour north of Stockholm, it offers proximity to the city center without being engulfed in the bustle. The extraordinarily low corporation tax rates in Solna have resulted in a booming job economy. Several well-known attractions in Solna are Friends Arena, Westfield Mall of Scandinavia, Hagaparken, Mulle Meck-parken, and Filmstaden.

This article is going to provide you with a list of 7 thrilling activities in Solna, as we guide you to make the most of your visit to this dynamic city.

7. Friends Arena – Sporting Spectacles

  • Address: Råsta Strandväg 1, 169 56 Solna, Sweden
  • Phone: +46 8 502 535 00

Nationalarenan is a multipurpose stadium in Stockholm, Sweden with a retractable roof. Due to sponsorship, it is currently known as Friends Arena. It is the largest stadium in Scandinavia and is situated in Solna, just north of the City Centre, near to the lake Råstasjön.

Sweden’s national arena, Friends Arena, is among the world’s most contemporary multi-arenas. You get to witness the best moments thanks to unobstructed views from every seat, proximity to the arena floor, and the newest in communication technology.

The Arena can quickly transition between managing events on grass, gravel, ice, and parquet flooring. Regardless of the weather outside, we can host sporting events and concerts all year long because of the retractable roof.

AIK Fotboll and the Swedish men’s national football team both play their home games at Friends Arena. The Arena has held two significant UEFA events, the UEFA Women’s Euro 2013 in front of a record audience and the UEFA Europa League Final between Ajax and Manchester United in 2017. The Arena complies with UEFA and FIFA rules for international football.

Friends Arena has held performances by Beyoncé, Coldplay, Guns N’ Roses, Eminem, Ed Sheeran, Bruce Springsteen, AC/DC, Iron Maiden, and Swedish House Mafia despite its youth.

6. Haga Park – Royal Serenity

  • Address: Hagalund, Solna, Sweden
  • Hours: Open 24 hours
  • Phone: +46 8 402 60 00

Situated in the district of Solna, north of Stockholm, lies the verdant Haga Park, sometimes known as Haga, which is a part of the Royal National City Park. Established between 1780 and 1797 by King Gustav III of Sweden, Haga is one of the country’s most well-liked leisure destinations and the best example of an English landscape park in Sweden.

There are also a lot of historical landmarks there. The park is home to the Chinese Temple, the Turkish Kiosk, and the Royal Burial Ground. Haga Palace, the home of Crown Princess Victoria and her family, is also located within the park.

Haga Palace, King Gustav III’s Pavilion, Chinese Pavilion, Haga Echo Temple, Turkish Kiosk, an older castle ruin (which is the remains of a never-finished castle), and a few other intriguing buildings on the grounds (like the unusual Copper Tents and Butterfly House) are all located within the park.

The Royal Burial Ground of the Swedish Royal family, which has been a part of Haga Park since 1922, is home to a number of the current Swedish royal Bernadotte family’s forebears and members.

5. Mall of Scandinavia – Shopper’s Paradise

  • Address: Stjärntorget 13 C, 169 79 Solna, Sweden
  • Hours: Closes 9 pm
  • Phone: +46 8 400 080 00

A retail center called Westfield Mall of Scandinavia is situated in Solna, Stockholm, Sweden. With 168 stores and an opening date of November 12, 2015, it is the fourth largest mall in the Nordic region. Several of the businesses have double-height storefronts that reach a height of eight meters. 50,000 people attended the mall’s grand opening.

Visit Westfield Mall of Scandinavia, the biggest shopping mall in Stockholm and all of Scandinavia, for a lavish dining, shopping, and entertainment experience. This location boasts 224 shops and eateries including top-notch Swedish and global brands, global gastronomic experiences, and Sweden’s sole commercial IMAX® theater. The popular shopping mall is about 7 minutes away by car from Central Station in Stockholm.

Smaller labels showcase themselves in pop-up boutiques at the Designer Gallery, which was established among other reasons to draw in new clientele. The main mall, which spans 240 meters and houses many of the big international fashion companies with two stories and an eight-meter-tall façade, is the focal point of the retail complex.

4. Butterfly House Hagaparken – Tropical Escape

  • Located in: Hagaparken
  • Address: Hagaparken, 169 70 Solna, Sweden
  • Hours: Closes 4 pm
  • Phone: +46 8 730 39 81

Enter a beautiful, tropical setting where you can interact with hundreds of unique animals, including parrots, frogs, and butterflies. 1.2 million-liter saltwater aquarium containing reef fish and sharks. store and café in a greenhouse.

Encompassing more than 800 m², the butterfly house (Fjärilshuset) is located within Hagaparken. There are thousands of various types of butterflies breeding there. A sizable aviary, an Asian-inspired garden, and an ancient conservatory are also present. Exotic butterflies draw attention, like the Peacock Atlas, the largest moth in the world, which is found in the woods of Southeast Asia.

The butterfly house has unique circumstances that enable the butterflies to grow healthily. Throughout the day, the air temperature is consistently maintained above 25 degrees Celsius, and at night, it is decreased to 18 degrees. Another crucial factor is high air humidity. Experience the sensation of strolling through a tropical rainforest at Stockholm’s Butterfly House (Fjarilshuset) while you look for butterflies, parrots, koi, and lizards. Across a sizable greenhouse, 700 different species of butterflies call this wildlife center home.

Learn about the butterfly’s life cycle, from egg to larva to chrysalis to butterfly. The greenhouse maintains a suitable temperature throughout the year to facilitate transformation. Observe the emergence of butterflies, their feeding habits, and their mating rituals. Paths through the greenhouse’s tropical greenery lead to fish ponds and waterfalls. Daily feeding demonstrations of koi fish take place in the early afternoon. Keep an eye out for additional fauna, such as parrots perched in the tropical trees and lizards scurrying between the pools.

3. Bergshamra Kalkbrott – Quarry Adventure

Sweden’s Bergshamra suburb is located inside the Stockholm metropolitan region. It is located in Stockholm County’s Solna Municipality administratively. The Stocksundet Sea Strait to the northeast, the Ålkistan Channel to the southeast, and the Brunnsviken to the southwest encircle the suburb on three sides.

Bergshamra Kalkbrott is a unique limestone quarry. Its crystal-clear water and cliffs offers opportunities for swimming and having a refreshing dip in the quarry’s waters will be amazing.

2. Solna Centrum – Art in the Subway

  • Address: Solnavägen 13, 171 45 Solna, Sweden
  • Hours: Closes 7 pm
  • Phone: +46 8 128 434 60

A commercial center and metro station in the Solna Municipality, Solna Centrum is situated around 5 km (3.1 mi) from the heart of central Stockholm, Sweden. It opened on August 31, 1975, as part of the first section of the Blue Line between T-Centralen and Hjulsta, and it is located next to the Friends Arena. There are about 120 shops and eateries, 40 office buildings, and 214 residential units in the mall.

In 1975, Solna Centrum station opened with the first arm of the Blue Line. In addition to being classic in its beauty, the vivid green and red landscape the red representing the setting sun behind the treetops, and the green representing the forest is also politically charged, something that not many other stations’ artwork does.

Karl-Olov Björk and Anders Åberg, painters, believed that the walls were incomplete when they were finished. Thus, they kept enhancing the forest with new scenarios and subtleties.

“The walls were originally only meant to be green and red,” says Marie Andersson, the art guide. Therefore, the remaining paintings were essentially created on the spot. This led to the creation of images that represented some of the most hotly contested social issues in Sweden throughout the 1970s, including the environment, deforestation, and rural region population decline.

1. Karolinska Institute Nobel Museum – Scientific Exploration

  • Address: Solnavägen 1, 171 77 Solna, Sweden
  • Phone: +46 8 524 800 00

Located in Solna, Sweden, the Karolinska Institute is a medical university focused on research, part of the Stockholm metropolitan area. The Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine is given out by the Nobel Assembly at the Karolinska Institute. Fifty university professors representing a range of medical specialties make up the assembly.

Located in Gamla Stan, the old town in central Stockholm, Sweden, the Nobel Prize Museum is housed in the former Stock Exchange Building on the north side of the square Stortorget.

Located northwest of Stockholm, in the county of Stockholm, in east-central Sweden, is the city of Solna. Several Viking-era burial mounds and runic stones can be found in this old hamlet. A cathedral dating back to the 12th century, the Karlberg Palace, a military academy dating back to the 18th century, Ulriksdal Palace, which occasionally doubles as a royal palace, and a sports stadium with space for roughly 52,000 people are among the notable structures.

Hence, take time to explore the city’s sports, culture, nature, and shopping opportunities.
