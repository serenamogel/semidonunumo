Jello Shots Recipe (Customize Both Flavor and Color)

Yummy Jello Shots are the perfect way to celebrate St. Patrick’s Day or any party! This easy green jello shot recipe is a delicious blend of tart lime jello mixed with sweet apple vodka but really, any flavor of jello can be used. This easy recipe can be made ahead of time which makes jello shots perfect for parties and celebrations.

Jello Shot Recipe

St. Patrick’s Day doesn’t have to just be all green beer and Irish coffees. Bring some luck to the party with these St. Patrick’s Day Jello Shots! Simple and easy to make but full of sweet and tangy flavor.

Customize them for any day of the year by changing up the colors, flavors, and sprinkles you choose to use. Talk about magically delicious!

Why I Love Jello Shots

  • They’re so easy to make! Three ingredients is all it takes to make these strong and tasty little shots. Mix up a batch, pour into containers, let them set up and you’re done! So simple and so effective.
  • They’re fun and festive. Who doesn’t love a good themed drink at a celebration? I love the green color and the little sprinkle of gold sugar dusted over the whipped cream.
  • You can make them in advance. These shots can be stored for 4 days so you can easily make the batch ahead of time and then pop them out to serve.
  • Fun to customize. I’m going with green for St. Patrick’s Day but this jello shot recipe can be easily customized for any party or celebrations. Simply choose a different flavor of jello and sprinkles to match your theme.

    How To Make Jello Shots

  • Whisk together the lime gelatin (or whatever flavor you are using) and boiling water in a medium, heat-proof mixing bowl until gelatin has completely dissolved.
  • Add in the apple vodka (or flavor of your choice) and whisk to combine.
  • Fill each cup or shot glass about three-quarters full leaving a little room for whipped cream and/or sprinkles.
  • Refrigerate for 3 hours, until the Jello has set up completely.
  • Top each shot with whipped cream and gold sanding sugar (if making for St. Patrick’s Day) just before serving.

    • These little plastic cups are perfect for jello shots. It helps that they are disposable too. You can of course use real shot glasses but you will need to cover them if you have any leftovers. These come with little lids so they’re perfect! I keep these on hand for all sorts of things like snacks, condiments for lunches, etc.

    Storage Information

    Refrigerator: Keep the party going by storing any leftover Jello Shots in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Make sure each container has a lid or store them in a large container that can be sealed.

    Freezer: I don’t recommend freezing them.

    Jello Shot Recipe FAQs

    Can this recipe be doubled?

    Absolutely! This recipe begs to be doubled or even tripled to serve a whole party! After all, no one stops after just one Jello shot! 

    Can I use all vodka instead of water and vodka?

    Unfortunately, no. Making the jello as directed in the recipe card is imperative for it to set up properly. Vodka alone won’t solidify enough to give you the consistency you need for the shot. The water mixed into the gelatin is important to the process and needs to be kept in. Best to stick to the recipe in this case. 

    Can I use a different flavor of jello?

    Yes, you sure can! This This jello shot recipe works for all flavors of jello so use your favorite flavor and color to match your party’s theme!

    Trish’s Tips and Tricks

    • Add the whipped cream and sprinkles just before serving for best results.
    • Feel free to use plain vodka or any other flavor you want. I chose a “green flavor” to fit the theme. The same goes for the Jello. I chose green for the holiday but you can easily change the flavor and color to fit any occasion.
    • Do not add more than half a cup of vodka to the recipe. The jello won’t set properly if you add to much.
    • Swap out the gold sanding sugar for some festive St. Patrick’s Day sprinkles. Or any other kind of sprinkles/colored sugar to fit your theme.
