Early Life and Background

The Kid Laroi, born Charlton Kenneth Jeffrey Howard, is a rising star in the music industry and a proud Australian rapper. He was born on August 17, 2003, in Waterloo, New South Wales, and spent his childhood in the neighboring suburb of Redfern.

Growing up, Laroi was exposed to a variety of musical genres, including country, hip-hop, and rock. He was interested in music from a young age, and his mother, who was a speaker in the local Aboriginal community, often sang lullabies to him. Laroi’s grandfather was also a member of a band, which allowed him to get hands-on experience with instruments at a young age.

Unfortunately, Laroi was forced to mature quickly as he faced significant challenges in his personal life. At the age of six, he lost his father, who was killed in a traffic accident. To make ends meet, Laroi’s mother worked as a laborer and struggled to provide for the family.

Despite his challenging upbringing, Laroi remained committed to his musical aspirations. He began writing music at the age of 13 and released his first mixtape, 14 with a Dream, in 2018. This project helped him gain local recognition and set the stage for his future success.
